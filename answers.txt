Why is ensuring access to clean drinking water and sanitation important, and what are some challenges in achieving this goal?\nAnswer: Ensuring access to clean drinking water and sanitation is important because it is a basic human right and essential for health, well-being, and economic development. Lack of access to clean water and sanitation can lead to waterborne diseases, malnutrition, and overall poor health outcomes. Additionally, clean water is necessary for agriculture, industry, and overall environmental sustainability.

Some challenges in achieving this goal include water scarcity, especially in regions affected by climate change, inadequate infrastructure for water and sanitation services, lack of funding and resources, and unequal access to clean water and sanitation, particularly in marginalized communities and developing countries. Addressing these challenges requires coordinated efforts, investment in infrastructure, and sustainable water management practices.\n--------------------------------------------------\n\nQuestion: 8. This progress is commendable. However, challenges remain in ensuring sustainable energy for all. One major challenge is the continued rise in the global population, leading to more people needing affordable energy for various purposes. The reliance on fossil fuels for energy generation contributes to greenhouse gas emissions, which in turn exacerbates climate change. To address these challenges, there is a need to focus on energy efficiency, invest in clean energy sources like solar and wind, and adopt sustainable energy practices to meet electricity needs while protecting the environment.\n--------------------------------------------------\n\nQuestion: 9.
